# Code of Conduct

Last updated: 2026-05-07

Selftend is a free, non-profit, open-source mental health project. The people working on it deserve a calm, respectful space, and so do the people the product is meant to help.

This code applies to everyone in project spaces: GitHub (issues, PRs, discussions, code review), Discord, project email aliases, and any other channel that represents the project.

## Our standards

Behavior we welcome:

- patience and empathy, especially with first-time contributors
- thoughtful, specific feedback on work, not on people
- crediting others' contributions, including non-code work
- respect for different backgrounds, identities, languages, and experience levels
- care with mental-health-related language; remember that contributors and users may be living the things this app addresses

Behavior that is not acceptable:

- harassment, intimidation, or personal attacks
- discriminatory or demeaning comments related to age, body, disability, ethnicity, gender identity or expression, nationality, race, religion, sexual orientation, or socioeconomic status
- sexualized language, imagery, or unwelcome advances
- doxxing or sharing someone's private information without consent
- mockery of mental-health experiences, diagnoses, or coping strategies
- sustained disruption of discussion, design review, or contributor coordination
- using the project to promote products, services, or content that conflict with the mission (ads, paid recovery programs, AI therapist framing, etc.)

## Mental-health context

Contributors and users may be reading or writing this content while struggling. Default to kindness even when you disagree. Disagreement is fine; cruelty is not.

If a discussion drifts into someone disclosing personal distress or a crisis, do not improvise crisis support inside a GitHub thread. Acknowledge calmly, point them to the resources at [/crisis](https://selftend.org/crisis), and step back from the technical thread.

## Reporting

If you see or experience behavior that breaks this code, contact the maintainer privately:

- email: `support@selftend.org`

Reports are read by the project maintainer. Reports about the maintainer's own conduct should still be sent to the same address; if that is not workable, raise the issue publicly in a GitHub issue and the broader contributor base can weigh in.

When you report, include what happened, where (link to issue, PR, message, channel), and roughly when. Anything you ask to be kept confidential will be kept confidential, except where doing so would prevent us from acting on the report.

## Enforcement

The maintainer may take any of the following steps, scaled to the situation:

- private clarification of the issue
- public correction in the relevant thread
- editing or removing offending comments, commits, code, or other contributions
- temporary or permanent ban from project spaces
- reporting to platform abuse teams (GitHub, Discord, etc.) where appropriate

Decisions will be explained, but the maintainer is not obligated to debate every enforcement action publicly.

## Scope

This code applies to all project spaces and to any individual representing the project in public — for example, using a project handle on social platforms, speaking on the project's behalf, or acting as a maintainer in another community.

It does not extend to people's unrelated personal conduct outside project spaces, unless that conduct is directed at members of the project community or materially affects their safety inside it.
